Projects

⏵ Responsive portfolio Website

Homepage: The first page visitors see. About Me: A page dedicated to my background, skills, and experience. Projects: A showcase of my best work. Include high-quality images, descriptions, and links to live projects if applicable. Skills: A list of my key skills, often displayed with icons or progress bars. This can include technical skills, software proficiency, and soft skills. Contact: A way for visitors to get in touch with me.

⏵ Contact Form

Form Fields: Name: A field for the visitor to enter their name. Email: A field for the visitor to enter their email address. Subject: A field for the visitor to enter the subject of their message (optional). Message: A larger text area for the visitor to write their message. Submit Button: A button to submit the form Validation: Basic validation to ensure required fields are filled out and that the email address is in the correct format.

⏵ Photo Gallery

High-Quality Images: Use high-resolution images to ensure your work looks its best. Consistent Aspect Ratio: Keep your images the same size and aspect ratio for a neat, organized look. Responsive Design: Ensure your gallery looks good and functions well on all devices. Lazy Loading: Load images as they are needed to improve page load times. Accessibility: Include alt text for images to improve accessibility and SEO.

⏵ Dashboard Layout

Header: Typically includes the title of the dashboard, a logo, and possibly user profile information and navigation links. Sidebar: Provides navigation options to switch between different sections or pages of the dashboard. Main Content Area: Displays the primary information and interactive elements such as charts, tables, forms, and widgets. Footer: Contains supplementary information such as copyright notices, links to policies, or additional navigation.

⏵ Product Cards

Creating product cards for an e-commerce site or a portfolio is a great way to display items attractively and informatively. Product cards typically include images, titles, descriptions, prices, and action buttons (like "Add to Cart" or "View Details").Image: A photo of the product. Title: The name of the product. Description: A short description of the product. Price: The price of the product. Action Button: Buttons for actions like adding to cart or viewing details.

⏵ Pricing Cards

Creating pricing cards is a great way to showcase different service plans or product pricing tiers on a website. Pricing cards should be visually appealing, easy to read, and clearly distinguish between different pricing options.Title: The name of the plan or pricing tier. Price: The cost of the plan. Features: A list of features or benefits included in the plan. Call to Action: A button for actions like "Sign Up" or "Buy Now." Highlight: Optionally, highlight a recommended or popular plan.
